Q: What does the "Risk Level" button do?

A: The Risk Level determines the bot's effective leverage.

The long answer: The wallet exposure limit defines the maximum ratio of position size to account balance that the bot can use. For instance, if the total wallet exposure limit is set to 2.0 while trading four different coins, the exposure per coin would be (2.0 / 4) = 0.5 or 50%. If trading COIN at $1.00 with a wallet balance of $1,000, the maximum allowable position size would be (0.5 * $1,000) / $1.00 = 500 COIN.

Note: A higher wallet exposure limit increases risk. For long positions, an exposure limit above 1.0 raises the risk of liquidation, while short positions carry liquidation risk at any exposure level.

Q: What does the "Unstuck" button do?

A: Passivbot manages underperforming, or "stuck", positions by realizing small losses over time. If multiple positions are stuck, the bot prioritizes the one with the smallest gap between its entry price and the current market price for unsticking. Losses are limited by ensuring that the account balance does not drop below a set percentage of its past peak.
