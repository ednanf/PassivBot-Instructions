Q: What does the "Risk Level" button do?

A: The risk button allows users to set the Total Wallet Exposure (TWE). TWE determines how much of the current funds the bot can use.

For instance, BrahmaBot currently has its default risk set to TWE 2 and its leverage set to 10x*. This means 20% of your funds are actively traded by the bot, while the remaining 80% serves as support if the price moves against your position.

With TWE 2, liquidation occurs at approximately a 50% drop from the average position price.

As another example, TWE 10 represents the entire deposit. At this level, liquidation happens if the price moves against the position by about 8-9%.

Most users set TWE between 2 and 5, with 5 being highly aggressive and risky. You can adjust your TWE separately for long and short positions, but remember: a higher TWE means a greater risk of liquidation!

*Different leverage levels change the calculations accordingly.

Q: What does the "Unstuck" button do?

A: Passivbot manages underperforming, or "stuck", positions by realizing small losses over time. If multiple positions are stuck, the bot prioritizes the one with the smallest gap between its entry price and the current market price for unsticking. Losses are limited by ensuring that the account balance does not drop below a set percentage of its past peak.
