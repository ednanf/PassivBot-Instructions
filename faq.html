Q: What does the "Risk Level" button do?

A: The Risk Level determines, in short, it is the bot's leverage.

The long answer: The wallet exposure limit defines the maximum ratio of position size to account balance that the bot can use. This limit can exceed 1.0 (or 100%) when leverage is applied. For instance, if the total wallet exposure limit is set to 2.0 while trading four different coins, the exposure per coin would be (2.0 / 4) = 0.5 or 50%. If trading COIN at $1.00 with a wallet balance of $1,000, the maximum allowable position size would be (0.5 * $1,000) / $1.00 = 500 COIN.

ATTENTION: a higher wallet exposure limit increases risk. For long positions, an exposure limit above 1.0 raises the risk of liquidation. Short positions, however, carry liquidation risk at any exposure level.

Q: What does the "Unstuck" button do?

A: Quoting PassivBot's instructions:

"Passivbot manages underperforming, or 'stuck', positions by realizing small losses over time. If multiple positions are stuck, the bot prioritizes positions with the smallest gap between the entry price and current market price for "unstucking". Losses are limited by ensuring that the account balance does not fall under a set percentage below the past peak balance."

Q: Can I trade manually in the same sub-account?

A: It is best to refrain from doing so. The bot calculates your current funds to place positions, and if you constantly mess with it, the bot may not operate properly.

You should also avoid placing positions, modifying take profits, etc. manually. Let the bot work alone!
